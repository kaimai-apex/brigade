## Equity: common frameworks (context, not prescription)

- **Two-cofounder splits** commonly land somewhere between an even 50/50 and a weighted split reflecting idea origination, full-time commitment, capital, and who's building the core asset. A purely technical cofounder who builds the entire product from scratch typically commands a substantial share — frequently near-parity in early-stage software companies — precisely because the product *is* the company at this stage.
- **The honest framing for Kai:** "for a portion of equity" (Jordan's phrasing) can mean a lot of things. A technical cofounder building the whole platform is usually negotiating in the *meaningful-double-digits* range, not a small advisory slice. What's "fair" depends on full-time vs part-time, who's funding costs, and idea origination — but be wary of any structure that treats the person building the entire product as a minor equity holder.
- **Don't anchor on a number from one text message.** Have the explicit conversation: full-time or not? Salary or sweat-only? Who pays the ~$0 (Vercel/Supabase free tiers) → low monthly costs early?

## Vesting (do this regardless of split)

- **Standard:** 4-year vest with a 1-year cliff, for *every* founder including Jordan. This protects everyone — if any cofounder leaves in month 3, they don't walk with a huge chunk while the others carry the company for years.
- Acceleration terms (on acquisition) are a later/lawyer conversation.

## Decision rights & structure

- Define who has final say on what (product direction, technical decisions, spend) before a disagreement forces it.
- Form a proper entity (LLC or C-corp depending on jurisdiction + fundraising plans) with a written founders' agreement and IP assignment — **all IP Kai builds must be assigned to the company, not held personally.**
- A simple operating agreement + cap table + vesting schedule is cheap now and priceless later.
