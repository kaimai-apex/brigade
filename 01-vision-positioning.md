# 01 — Vision & Positioning

## Mission

Give independent private chefs the professional infrastructure that salaried professionals take for granted — a credible public identity, the tools to run their business, and a network that sends them work — so they spend less time hustling and more time cooking.

## The positioning sentence

> **Brigade is where private chefs build their reputation, run their business, and get found — the LinkedIn and the back-office for the private hospitality world.**

Two halves, both essential:

- **The credibility layer (LinkedIn-side):** a verified, beautiful, SEO-discoverable profile that a chef is *proud* to send to their own leads. This is the hook — it's useful even with zero other users.
- **The tools layer (Workspace-side):** inquiry inbox, availability calendar, quote/proposal builder, invoicing & deposits, menu builder, client CRM. This is the retention — once a chef runs their bookings through Brigade, leaving means losing their workflow.

The strategic trick: **the credibility layer makes the chef want to drive their *own* off-platform leads to their Brigade profile.** That converts the disintermediation problem into a growth loop — every off-platform client a chef sends to their profile is a free demand-side acquisition for us.

## Who we serve

### Primary user (supply) — the independent private chef
- 3–10 years' experience, runs their own private-chef business (or wants to).
- Pain: inconsistent bookings, marketing they hate doing, no-shows/cancellations, looking unprofessional next to agencies, juggling quotes and invoices in their notes app and Instagram DMs.
- They already have *some* clients but no system. They are not looking for a restaurant job — they're running a micro-business badly-supported by tools.
- **This is the wedge user.** Win them first, everything else follows.

### Primary buyer (demand) — the people who hire private chefs
Ranked by willingness to pay and recurrence:
1. **Estate / household managers & family offices** — hire repeatedly, value vetting, currently pay agencies 15–25% of salary. Highest LTV.
2. **Villa / yacht / luxury short-term rental operators** — recurring seasonal need for vetted chefs.
3. **Catering & events agencies** — need freelance chefs to staff gigs.
4. **Affluent households** — one-off and recurring private dining. Highest volume, lowest individual value, best SEO target.

### Future users (expansion)
Sommeliers, private servers/waitstaff, butlers, mixologists, event/production staff, private nannies-who-cook, etc. Same profile + tools + directory mechanics. **Do not build for these on day one** — they are Phase 12+.

## The wedge (how we get in)

Pick the narrowest version that's still a business:

> **Independent private chefs in [ONE metro], plus the estate managers / agencies / affluent households who hire them in that metro.**

Geography matters because (a) the directory has to be locally dense to be useful to a buyer, and (b) word-of-mouth among chefs is intensely local. Pick a single affluent, chef-dense metro (e.g. London, LA, Miami, NYC, or a specific wealthy region) and saturate it before expanding. One city with 300 active, paying chefs beats ten cities with 30 each.

## What we are NOT (scope discipline)

- **Not a booking marketplace.** We don't take a cut of the meal. (Optional payments rail comes much later, opt-in, value-added — never mandatory commission.)
- **Not a restaurant job board.** Culinary Agents / Hosco / Poached own restaurant + hotel W-2 hiring. We are private/independent hospitality. Don't fight them on their turf.
- **Not a recruitment agency.** We're self-serve software + directory, not a high-touch human placement service (that's the incumbent we undercut).
- **Not multi-vertical on day one.** Private chefs only until the model is proven.

## Brand voice

Premium but not stuffy; built *by people who respect the craft.* Chefs are proud, skilled, often burned by exploitative platforms — the brand should feel like it's on their side (more pay, more control, ownership of their reputation), echoing what resonated for Yhangry's chef supply story. Avoid "gig economy" framing; lean into "professional / craftsperson / your business, leveled up."

## North-star metric

**Number of chefs who run at least one real booking per month *through* Brigade tools.** Not signups, not page views — chefs who've made Brigade their operating system. That's the number that predicts revenue, retention, and defensibility.
